The choosen dataset was the "U.S. National Epidemiological Survey on Alcohol and Related Conditions (NESARC)".

The topic of interest is about alcohol dependence associated with family history of alcoholism.

Our question is: is family history of alcoholism most significant in alcohol dependent people? Can it be used to define a risk group?

Our hypothesis is that we can predict and define a risk group for alcohol dependence with a reasonable accuracy using family history of alcohol abuse.

Looking for the literature about it, i've looked for "family profile of alcohol dependence". It's a largely studied theme: what are the characteristics that influents people to become alcohol dependent. We can see some examples of works in this way: "Substance use, abuse and dependence in adolescence: prevalence, symptom profiles and correlates", "Risk and protective factors for alcohol and other drug problems in adolescence and early adulthood: Implications for substance abuse prevention.", and Childhood Personality Predicts Alcohol Abuse in Young Adults.

In (Hawkings et al, 1992), many risk factors are identified in their conclusion, including family aspects. Here, we'd investigate the family history about dependencies significance.

These works and others usually try to find and define a risk group, for focused policies on them, and predictive power using data to find potential dependents. Our study here will follow this same objective: can we predict and/or distinguish risk groups?

It's not easy to define all the data we're going to use without taking a look in it, but we expect to use the following sections from the entire Codebook:

  • SECTION 1: BACKGROUND INFORMATION
  • SECTION 2A: ALCOHOL CONSUMPTION
  • SECTION 2B: ALCOHOL ABUSE/DEPENDENCE (ALCOHOL EXPERIENCES)
  • SECTION 2C: ALCOHOL TREATMENT UTILIZATION
  • SECTION 2D: FAMILY HISTORY (I) OF ALCOHOLISM
  • SECTION 3E: FAMILY HISTORY (II) OF DRUG ABUSE
